Online service for keyboard testing

You can perform an online typing test on your keyboard.

This may be necessary in a number of cases:


  1. After replacing the keyboard on a laptop, it is recommended to test each key to identify faulty buttons.

  2. Check the operation of all keyboard buttons when buying a used laptop.

  3. After cleaning the keyboard or repairing the mechanism, you must check the operation of the F1 to F12 keys, as well as the auxiliary keys Insert, home, end, PrtSc, PgUp, PgDn and other buttons that are difficult to check in a standard text document.

  4. Ensure that the keyboard is properly connected to the laptop and there are no visible signs of damage to wires or connectors.

  5. Check the operation of keys with functional combinations, such as Ctrl, Alt and Shift, as well as their combinations with other keys. Ensure that these keys work correctly.

  6. Ensure that the keys in the numbers and symbols section work properly, and all characters are displayed correctly.

  7. Check for the absence of "delays" between pressing a key and entering a character on the screen.

  8. Perform a test of the "Caps Lock" and "Num Lock" keys to make sure they turn on and off properly.

  9. Check the keyboard shortcut to bring up the system "Start" menu (if present) to make sure it functions.

  10. Test the "Delete" or "Backspace" key to see how they affect the entered text.

  11. Test the "Enter" or "Return" key, which is used to confirm the entered text.

  12. Ensure that the arrow keys work correctly and can move the cursor in text documents and on web pages.

What to do if water gets on the keyboard?

If liquid spills on your laptop keyboard, take the following steps:
- Turn off the laptop immediately and disconnect it from the power source.
- Shake off excess liquid, but do not shake the laptop too hard to avoid damage.
- Turn the laptop upside down so the liquid can drain out.
- Do not turn on the laptop until you are sure the entire component is completely dry.
- If the liquid contained sugar or other dissolved substances, it may be advisable to contact professionals for cleaning.
Always be careful with liquid near electronics, as it can cause serious damage.


Why do some keys not work or work incorrectly?

The most common causes are physical damage, dirt or liquid getting under the keys, and driver issues. Our test will help determine if the problem is mechanical.


What is key "sticking" and how to detect it?

"Sticking" or shorting occurs when a key does not return to its original position or continues to send a signal. In our test, such a key will remain highlighted in blue even after you release it.


Do I need to install testing software?

No, our service works completely online in your browser. You don't need to download or install anything, which makes the testing process fast and safe.



The online keyboard testing service KeyTest allows you to visually verify whether your computer keyboard is working properly or if some keys are working incorrectly or not working at all.
By opening the site keytest.pp.ua in a browser, you can immediately start testing, no programs need to be downloaded.
Start pressing the keys sequentially; the buttons currently pressed will be displayed in blue on the screen. Already tested ones will be gray (meaning the button was previously pressed and then released, so it is working).
If before you even start testing there are blue keys on the screen - they are most likely shorted (probably spilled with water), and the keyboard will have to be replaced. If when pressing one button, two or more are marked in blue - this also indicates a malfunction of the laptop keyboard.
